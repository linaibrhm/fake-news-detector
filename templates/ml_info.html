Definition Of Fake News

Fake news refers to false or misleading information presented as news, often created to influence public opinion, generate confusion, or achieve specific political or financial gains.

The Importance Of This Web App

In real-life scenarios, fake news can have significant consequences, shaping public perception and potentially leading to societal unrest, economic instability, or harm to individuals' reputations.

How Does It Work?

How Is Accuracy Calculated?

Accuracy is one of the most basic evaluation metrics in machine learning. It measures how often the model’s predictions are correct.

Formula:


Accuracy = (True Positives + True Negatives) / Total Predictions

For example, if the system correctly identified 90 real news and 85 fake news out of 200 articles, the accuracy would be:
(90 + 85) / 200 = 87.5% While accuracy is useful, it’s often used alongside other metrics such as precision, recall, and F1-score to give a fuller picture—especially when datasets are imbalanced.

Why Use Machine Learning Instead of Manual Checking?

  • Speed: ML models can analyze thousands of articles in seconds, unlike human fact-checkers.
  • Consistency: Unlike people, ML models apply the same logic every time—reducing subjective bias.
  • Scalability: As misinformation grows, automated systems can handle the volume much more efficiently than manual reviewers.
  • Learning from Data: ML models continuously improve as more data becomes available.
  • 24/7 Availability: Automated systems can operate non-stop without fatigue or human limitations.
